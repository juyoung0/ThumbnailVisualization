Introduction

Your faithful participation will help us establish a theory about good practices in using visualizations for data-driven journalism.

The goal of this experiment is to evaluate thumbnails designed for news articles.

This experiment is confirmed by IRB (UNISTIRB-18-52-C). Click below button to see the IRB approval document.


Please acknowledge the following restrictions before participating in our experiment. If you do not satisfy these restrictions, you will NOT receive compensation for your participation. Please read them carefully.

  • You must use a display with a sufficiently high resolution (minimum 1024 X 768). If your display has an insufficient display resolution, you may not proceed.
  • You can use a personal desktop or laptop computer. Participation with tablets, smart phones, or other mobile devices is not permitted.
  • You may be asked to maximize your browser window if its initial size is insufficient for participation.

Tutorial

  1. In this experiment, you will describe a thumbnail designed for a news article about a current topic in politics or economics.
  2. When you begin the experiment, you will be asked to complete a demographic survey.
  3. After the survey, you will be shown a thumbnail designed for a news article and evaluate how helpful the thumbnail is for you to guess the content of the article. Your responses will be entered as scores ranging from 1 to 7, with 7 being the highest score.
  4. With the given thumbnail, you will be asked to write a prediction of the contents of the article in 300 characters or less (minimum 70 characters).
  5. You will then be shown the actual news article associated with the thumbnail. You will read the article in 3 minutes and answer a simple question. The answer to this simple question can be found in the contents of the article, so please read the article thoroughly. IF YOU ANSWER INCORRECTLY, YOUR PARTICIPATION IN THE EXPERIMENT WILL END AND YOUR COMPENSATION WILL BE PRO-RATED.
  6. You will see then revisit the prediction that you wrote in c to evaluate again after you read the article. After an evaluation on the helpfulness of the thumbnail, you will be asked to answer 7 short questions about the news article, the thumbnail, and your initial description. YOUR ANSWER MUST BE WRITTEN IN ENGLISH TO RECEIVE COMPENSATION.
  7. Finally, you will evaluate helpfulness of the thumbnail again.
  8. After completing steps c. to g., you will be provided with a token number. You must take note of this token number and enter it into it to the HIT on Amazon Mechanical Turk. Supplying a valid token is necessary for your compensation.
  9. Your prediction will be evaluated by other Mechanical Turk workers. We will provide additional compensation ($1) for predictions that are deemed to be a high quality (top 25% answers). Conversely, if your prediction is deemed to be of low quality, your compensation will be prorated.
